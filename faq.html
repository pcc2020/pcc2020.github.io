e-PCC Seminar Series

We hope that the following provides answers to questions you may have about the ePCC seminar! If you have a question that is not answered here, please do not hesitate to contact one of the organisers, whose contact details are on the right-hand side of the homepage.

Who is the ePCC seminar series for?

For PhD students and postdocs working in all areas of combinatorics, discrete mathematics and complexity theory.

Does my talk have to contain new results?

No - talks that present partial results or provide an introduction to / survey of an area are also encouraged!

How long must my talk be?

Your talk should last at least 20 minutes, though speakers have an hour slot that they may fill.

Will my talk be recorded?

We will ask whether you would like your talk to be recorded and whether you would like the recording to be published.

Where can the recording of my talk be found?

Given your permission, we will host a recording of your talk on the ePCC seminar YouTube channel.

* Lean programming workshop

Prof. Kevin Buzzard

Lean is a computer proof checker. If a mathematician learns Lean's language then they will be able to type mathematical proofs into the system, and Lean will check that they are complete and correct. During the workshop we will give you a working knowledge of how to use Lean to do undergraduate level mathematics, and we will finish by looking at Lean proofs of MSc level combinatorics such as the Kruskal Katona theorem. No previous experience with Lean is required, but of course the better prepared you are on the day, the more you will get out of the workshop. If you only try one thing beforehand, you might want to make it the natural number game (link below).

Relevant links:

Invited speakers

The keynote speakers for PCC2020 are:

Dr. Julia Böttcher

Julia is an Associate Professor at the London School of Economics and Political Science. Her research focuses on extremal graph and hypergraph theory (in particular embedding and packing problems), Ramsey theory, as well as random and pseudorandom discrete structures. She likes frisbees, tango, her three loud children, and good excuses to visit Brazil.

Prof. Kevin Buzzard

Kevin Buzzard is an algebraic number theorist and a professor of pure mathematics at Imperial College London. He thinks that the next generation of mathematicians should be using computer proof checkers like this generation uses LaTeX.

Dr. Jess Enright

Jess Enright is a lecturer in computing science at the University of Glasgow. Her main research interests are in graph theory and network epidemiology, but she’s also a big fan of combinatorial games. After a theoretical PhD on algorithmic graph theory, she branched out to practical epidemiology, and now spends time working between the practical and the theoretical. For a brief time, she was literally the world’s worst tiddlywinks player.

Bhavik Mehta

Prof. Nik Ruškuc

Nik's interests lie in combinatorial algebra and algebraic combinatorics. His early work was on semigroup presentations - and hence combinatorics of words - which remains an ongoing fascination. More recently he has branched into combinatorics of permutation pattern involvement. In both strands of activity applications of theoretical computer science - automata, languages, decidability - underpin his investigations.
